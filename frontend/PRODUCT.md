# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Angolan individuals looking for credit and insurance products. They have moderate digital literacy, use the web on both mobile and desktop, and need clear, trustworthy guidance through a financial decision process. The experience should reduce anxiety, not add to it — these are high-stakes choices about real money.

Secondary users: financial institution representatives (provedores) managing their service listings, and platform administrators monitoring usage, users, and ML model performance.

## Product Purpose

SRFS (Sistema de Recomendação de Serviços Financeiros) helps Angolan users find suitable financial services — credit and insurance — by matching their financial profile to available products using machine learning. Each recommendation comes with a plain-language explanation so users understand *why* a product fits them. The system learns from feedback to improve over time. Success is measured by users finding and acting on financial products that genuinely serve their needs.

## Brand Personality

**Confident · Clear · Human**

A trusted advisor who speaks plainly, not a corporate bank. Warm and approachable but never sloppy; precise and data-driven but never cold. The interface should feel like guidance from someone competent who has your best interest in mind.

Voice: direct, supportive, unpretentious. Empathetic without being saccharine. Professional without being rigid.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards. No cookie-cutter admin template feel.
- Cold corporate banking UIs. No navy-on-white sterility, no marble-and-glass affectation.
- Overly playful or gamified fintech. Financial decisions deserve seriousness.

The brand should feel distinctively *its own* — warm but competent, digital-native but trustworthy.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through clarity.** Financial decisions are high-stakes. Every screen should reduce uncertainty: show the user exactly where they stand, why a recommendation fits, and what to do next. Ambiguity is the enemy.

2. **Human-first fintech.** Warm, approachable, never cold or corporate. The interface should feel like a conversation with a trusted advisor — not a bank vault or a spreadsheet.

3. **Guidance over features.** Lead the user through the recommendation flow. Don't dump them into a dashboard and expect them to figure it out. Every screen has one clear action.

4. **Earned complexity.** Keep the simple path simple. Add detail and controls only when the user needs to make a decision, investigate an option, or dig deeper. Progressive disclosure, not everything-at-once.

5. **Confident simplicity.** Every screen answers one question and offers one next step. Remove anything that competes with the primary task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. Color contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support throughout. Reduced motion respected. The system should be usable by Angolan users across varying digital literacy levels, devices, and connection quality.
